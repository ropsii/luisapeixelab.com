Identification of the risk factors promoting the emergence of Enterococcus faecium at the interphase between humans, foodborne animals and the environment

Goal: To unveil the pathogenic potential of E. faecium populations present in human and non-human hosts and to improve the identification of the factors contributing to their pathogenic potential.

Identification of the risk factors promoting the emergence of Enterococcus faecium: a combined approach of conventional methods and high-throughput technology

Developments on FTIR-ATR, MALDI-TOF MS and Raman for rapid detection of relevant bacteria

Goal: To optimize methodologies based on spectroscopic techniques (FTIR-ATR/MALDI-TOF MS/Raman) for routine identification of clinically relevant bacterial species (Acinetobacter / Enterobacteriaceae / Enterococcus), serotypes and clones.
